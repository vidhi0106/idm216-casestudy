The overview

In this project we created a responsive web based application for a foodtruck called Tyson Bees. The problem was that the foodtruck did not have any way for the customers to place an order online or in advance so we created this app to facilitate ordering online from the food truck. We carried out multiple usability testings with low fidelity wireframes to test out the features and map out the experience, high fidelity designs to get more feedback on the aesthetics and finally, we tested our final website to get feedback on the final functionality of the application. All of these usability test sessions helped us make informed design and development decisions to provide the best user experience. Through our usability tests we realized that we did not need a filter button for the menu and that people preferred a simplistic design with an app flow that was clean and had minimal clicks.

Context and Challenge

Tyson Bees is a foodtruck located on Univesity of Pennsylvania's campus that serves a variety of different dishes. The project was spanned across 11 weeks and was carried out with a team of 5 people ( Anuj, Gianna, Samiha, Clay and Vidhi ). Each member of the team had a specific role and I was the Designer and Data Architect for this project. As the designer, I was responsible for designing the screens for different sizes like mobile, tablet and desktop and carry out the usability test to get feedback on the same and as the data architect I was responsible for maintaining the files and ensuring everything is documented and easy to locate for all members of the team.

Purpose: To deliver a web based application to allow users to order food from Tyson Bees online.
Problem:Tyson Bees did not have an online platform to facilitate online ordering and allowing customers to see the menu, truck location, hours of operation and track their rewards at all times.
Goals and Objectives: The goal was to create a functional application that would allow users to login/signup, place orders, track their orders and track rewards for their past orders. The first determinant of success was for the user to be able to carry out all the goals successfully and have a pleasant experience ordering off the app. We based our success on the results we got from the usability tests where asked our participants their thoughts about the design and experience with the app. A positive result proved that we were successful with the project.

Process and Insight

We started off by creating user personas and mood boards to brainstorm the different ideas we had for the visuals of the application. We also created a journey map to map the userâ€™s journey and map out pain points and problems.

Next, we created low fidelity wireframes for different screen size to map out the different features and functions of the app.

The designs were later revised based on the feedback we received from the usability tests. I tried out different approaches to the design and played around with color schemes and graphics to push a brand identity and make the designs more continuous and purposeful. I also worked to make the changes in the design based on the feedback we received from the usability session. The main screens that I changed were the order category/ menu page ( to remove the side scroll), added an order history page and made the design more sleek and streamlined in general.

We then coded out the design using HTML and CSS. I helped with coding the initial build of the web app. I was also responsible for creating the usability test script to get feedback on the designs which is both qualitative and quantitative. I then carriued out multiple usability tests which helped us with iterating different versions of the website and hear more from the users about their preferences.

The final project was then put together by the developers to add the functionality to the existing designs, maintain the database to allow users to pick from anything in the menu and make the app fully functional and add microinteractions to give a better user experience.

The solution

The final website had a categories section for easy navigation to different sub categories, we also had a hamburger menu to navigate to pages like rewards and order history. We had a cart icon in the top so that the user can checkout easily. The app design used a lot of hexagons to reinforce the brand identity. We also included microinteractions for item quantity and deleting an item from the cart.
